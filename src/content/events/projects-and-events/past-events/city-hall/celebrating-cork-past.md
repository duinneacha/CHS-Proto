<h1>Projects & Events</h1>
    <h2>Celebrating Cork Past Exhibition</h2>
    <h3>Our first attendance</h3>
    <figure class="FigureRight">
        <img src="/assets/Events/Projects%20And%20Events/Past%20Events/City%20Hall/Richard%20Cooke.jpg" alt="ImageRichardCooke">
    <figcaption>L-R: Jim Barry (Chair), André Saubolle (Secretary), Richard Cooke (Coordinator - Cork City Council)</figcaption>
    </figure>
    <p class="indentedText">
        The annual <a href="http://celebratingcorkpast.com/" target="_blank">Celebrating Cork Past Exhibition</a> 
        is a joint project sponsored by both City and County Councils, with the Lyon’s Club, held at Cork’s City Hall.</p>
    <p class="indentedText">
        Welcomed by coordinator, Richard Cooke the previous day, we were fortunate to have been able to setup our stall in 
        a prime location directly opposite the main entrance, so in an ideal position to be the first to welcome visitors
        to the exhibition the following day. Richard was a frequent visitor to our exhibits on the day. Like so many others
        he was particularly interested in our de Barra items.</p>
    <p class="indentedText">
        Despite the threat of miserable weather forecasted, our first attendance at the City Hall’s Exhibition, for us was a roaring success. 
    </p>
    <h3>Our Exhibits</h3>
    <figure class="FigureLeft">
        <img src="/assets/Events/Projects%20And%20Events/Past%20Events/City%20Hall/SigningVisitorsBook.JPG" alt="ImageSigningVisitor">
    </figure>
    <p class="indentedText">
        We greeted many friends of Carrigtwohill from home and some from overseas, who were extremely interested in discussing our exhibits,
        including the Lord Mayor of Cork, who signed our Visitor&#39;s book.</p>
    <p class="indentedText">
        It must be said that the feature attraction was <a href="/events/debarra/de-barra/">Dáibhí de Barra's</a> manuscript with transcribed poem used as a handout 
        for the joint unveiling of commemorative plaque ceremony and Paul Doherty’s returning manuscript to the village community
        earlier this year, gained the most interest. <a href="/events/projects-and-events/past-events/miriam-odonovan/miriam-o-donovan/">Miriam’s O&#39;Donovan&#39;s</a> presentation about his prayer book found at the Melbourne State Library, and the story of the Rossmore Tithe wars as reported by de Barra coming a close second. These two items
        were a perfect marriage.
    </p>
    <p class="indentedText">
        The next item of significant interest was the planned presentation marking coming 100<sup>th</sup> anniversary of the taking of <a href="/events/projects-and-events/coming-events/taking-of-the-barracks/takingof-barracks/">Carrigtwohill R.I.C. Barracks</a>, 3<sup>rd</sup> January 1820, to be given by one of our members, John (Bishop) O’Mahony.</p>
    <p class="indentedText">
        Also on display was a brief outline history of Mother Magdalen Taylor, founder of the <a href="/religious-of-parish/poor-servants/poor-servants/">Poor Servants of the Mother of God</a> whose order was established at Carrigtwohill, and is soon to be named Saint. In addition, was the brief outline history of <a href="/religious-of-parish/clergy/richard-seymour/richard-seymour/">Father Richard Seymour</a> who invited Mother Magdalen to the village in 1874.</p>
