<h1>Projects & Events</h1>
    <h2>Edward Bransfield of Ballinacurra & Antarctica</h2>
    <h3>Remembering Edward Bransfield Presentation</h3>
    <p class="indentedText">
        To help celebrate the bicentennial anniversary, and in an effort to create a better awareness within the 
        local community of Edward Bransfield (1784-1852) who hailed of Ballinacurra, we invited the 
        <a href="http://rememberingedwardbransfield.ie" target="_blank">Remembering Edward Bransfield</a> Committee
        Secretary, Liam O’Riordan (himself a Bransfield descendent), to outline the known history of Edward
        Bransfield.
    </p>
    <figure class="FigureRight">
        <img src="/assets/Events/Projects%20And%20Events/Past%20Events/Edward%20Bransfield/Liam-Michael.JPG" alt="">
    <figcaption>L-R: Liam O'Riordan (Remembering Edward Bransfield)
        <Br />thanks 
        <br />Michael White (Carrigtwohill & District)
    </figcaption>
    </figure>
    <p class="indentedText">
        On 9<sup>th</sup> November 2018, a bleak winter’s evening with many suffering from cold and sniffles,
        Liam gave an attentive audience his fascinating presentation in two parts. He began by documenting how,
        at the age of 18 this young man was ‘pressed’ into service for the Royal Navy; how he progressed through
        the ranks attaining his Ship’s Master status; his participation at the bombardment of Algiers, and how
        he bravely lead his ship during the battle to save the enslaved prisoners; leading to his historic discovery
        of the Continent of Antarctica, 30<sup>th</sup> January 1820; concluded by telling us how he ended his career as a
        Merchant Seaman. Liam also explained that there were no know surviving images of the man, and that his Ships’
        Log had mysteriously disappeared.</p>
    <p class="indentedText">
        Liam then explained how he was related to the Bransfield family of Cobh through his mother who was
        a Bransfield, and had traced them back to William Bransfield (1791 to 1853) a Shoemaker at the Holy
        Ground of what was then Cove. He advised that he had never been able to connect his family to the 
        Bransfield clans of Ballinacurra and Midleton.</p>
    <p class="indentedText">
        At the conclusion, as a committee we made a contribution to the Remembering Edward Bransfield fundraising
        efforts to erect a memorial at Ballinacurra to be unveiled on the 200<sup>th</sup> anniversary of his discovery. </p>
    <h3>Memorial Unveiling</h3>
    <p class="indentedText">
        We were honoured to have received a personal invitation to the unveiling ceremony of Edward Bransfield’s
        memorial at Ballinacurra on the 25<sup>th</sup> January 2020 where a number of our members were present.
    </p>
