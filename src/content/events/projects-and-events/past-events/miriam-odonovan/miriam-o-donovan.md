    <h1>Projects & Events</h1>
    <h2>Miriam O'Donovan Lecture</h2>
    <figure class="FigureRight">
        <img src="/assets/Events/Projects%20And%20Events/Past%20Events/Miriam%20O%27Donovan/Miriam%20presentation.jpg" alt="ImageMiriam">
    </figure>
    <p class="indentedText">
        As part of our society’s endeavours to learn more of Dáibhí de Barra, another success, and well attended event was held
        at the Community Centre, where UCC lecturer Miriam Uí Dhonnabháin, an Irish language scholar and singer whose particular
        interest is the study and preservation of old manuscripts, gave a fascinating lecture to a captive audience in relation
        to her studies into two of de Barra’s works; a number of whom were not local. One couple travelled from Limerick, while
        two descendants of Carrigtwohill, now living in Australia were also in attendance.
    </p>
    <p class="indentedText">
        Miriam told of how de Barra’s manuscripts are now being studied in universities by Irish language scholars and historians
        worldwide.
    </p>
    <figure class="FigureCenter">
        <img src="/assets/Events/Projects%20And%20Events/Past%20Events/Miriam%20O%27Donovan/Miriam%20audience.jpg" alt="ImageMiriamODonovan">
    </figure>
    <h3>Dáibhí de Barra Prayer Book</h3>
    <figure class="FigureRight">
        <img src="/assets/Events/Projects%20And%20Events/Past%20Events/Miriam%20O%27Donovan/Pray%20Book%20first%20leaf.jpg" alt="ImageFirstLeaf">
        <figcaption></figcaption>
    </figure>
    
    <p class="indentedText">
        The first part of Miriam's presentation was the telling of how de Barra’s prayer book was donated to the State Library of
        Melbourne by Rev John Barry D. D. of Watergrasshill, who was in Melbourne from 1856 to 1863 whose history while here was 
        shrouded in controversy. There is no evidence yet to suggest any relationship, although, it is generally believed very likely.
    </p>
    <h3>Dáibhí de Barra account of Tithe Wars battle at Rossmore</h3>
    <p class="indentedText">
        Miriam next told us of de Barra’s first hand account of the battle at Rossmore during the Tithe War era.
    </p>
