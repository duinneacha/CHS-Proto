    <h1>Projects & Events</h1>
    <h2>World War 1 Remembered</h2>
    <h3>Exhibition and lectures</h3>
    <p class="indentedText">
        One of our most successful events was held at the Carrigtwohill, hosted by the Community Council at Community Centre,
        21<sup>st</sup> November 2014, where Gerry White (Historian & Author) of the Western front Association (Cork Branch)
        with, Marie Fitzgerald, one of our own members, were our guest speakers for the event. </p>
    <p class="indentedText">
        Gerry gave a general overview of 
        the effects of the war on the men at the front and on the communities at home.</p>
    <p class="indentedText">
        Marie told the moving story of her grandfather, Daniel Fitzgerald, a native of the village, who was a stoker aboard
        <em><strong>HMS Tiger</strong></em> during the Battle of Jutland between 31<sup>st</sup> May 1916 & 1<sup>st</sup> June 1916. </p>
    <p class="indentedText">
        Daniel happily survived the war.</p>
    <p class="indentedText">
        The exhibits included; displays of weapons, uniforms, badges, medals, armoury, and other artefacts by;
    </p>
