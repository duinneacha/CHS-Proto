<h1>Families of Carrigtwohill</h1>
    <h2>Standish Barry of Leamlara</h2>
    
    <h3>Penal Laws - Oath of Allegiance</h3>

    <figure class="FigureRight">
        <img src="/assets/Persons%20of%20Note/Barry/Standish%20Barry/LeamlaraHouse.jpg" alt="ImageLeamlaraHouse">
    </figure>
    <p class="indentedText">
        The 'Convert Rolls' recorded those who converted to the so-called established faith. Whereas the 'Catholic Qualification
        Rolls' of the Penal Laws required Roman Catholics to take an oath of allegiance to the Crown in order to avail of
        relaxations of the Penal Laws, and benefit of rights newly restored under Catholic Relief Acts. These were predominantly
        prosperous landowners, merchants, and professionals, who had no intention of denouncing their faith.</p>
    <p class="indentedText">
        In 1774, this Act was passed to permit the King’s subjects, of any religion, to take an oath at the local assizes (courts) 
        “to testify to their loyalty and allegiance to him, to promote peace and industry in the kingdom”</p>
    <p class="indentedText">
        Standish Barry, signed the 'Catholic Qualification Rolls'. Thus, taking the oath, and remained Catholic.</p>
    <p class="IndentQuotes">
        “At an adjournment of a General Sessions of the peace held at the Kings Old Castle the 15 Febry 1794”</p>
  
   <h3>What's in a Name</h3>
    <p class="indentedText">
        Garret Barry of Leamlary, or Leamlara, obtained a ‘confirmatory' grant from, Charles II, for the princely sum 
        of £10, by deed dated 36<sup>th</sup> year of that sovereign’s reign, circa 1685.
    </p>
    <p class="indentedText">
        The following entry may be found within John Bernard Burke's, ‘A Genealogical and Heraldic History of the
        Commoners of Great Britain & Ireland’, 1836.</p>
    <p class="IndentQuotes">
        “Garrett married Ellen, daughter of Daniel Duf O’Cahill, by Ellen, his wife, daughter of M’Cartie Reagh,
        whose mother was descended from the noble house of Roche, Barrymore, and Ormond, by various intermarriages."</p>
    <figure class="FigureRight">
        <img src="/assets/Persons%20of%20Note/Barry/Standish%20Barry/Garrett%20Standish%20Barry%20baptism%2012%20June%201788.jpg" alt="ImageGSBarryBaptised">
        <figcaption>Garrett Standish Barry, 12<sup>th</sup>June 1788</figcaption>
    </figure>
    <p class="indentedText">
        Garrett who succeeded his father, married Catherine, daughter of Standish Grady, of Elton, Limerick. He 
        was in turn succeeded by Standish. Standish Barry who married Margaret Roche in July 1787, had six children
        who Burke names as; Garret Standish, John who died unmarried in 1833; Henry who married a Miss Burnett; Penelope,
        and Anne, all of whom adopted the double barrel name Standish Barry. <a href="/persons-of-note/barry/garrett-standish-barry/garrett-standish-barry/">Garrett Standish Barry</a>
        (1788–1864), grandson of Garrett & Mary Anne Hussey of Leamlara, succeeded his father according to Burke,
        in April 1823, was to inherit the family estate.</p>

    <h3>Death of Standish Barry</h3>
    <p class="indentedText">
        The opening passage of Burke’s genealogy of the Standish Barry family advises, Garrett Standish Barry, succeeded
        to the estate of Leamlara upon the death of his father, Standish, in April 1823. This was an incorrect statement,
        as he actually died two years earlier, 27<sup>th</sup> May 1821. An edited extract cleaned up version of the 
        Freeman Journal which announced his death has been included.  
    </p>
