High up in the townland of Woodstock, Bun a Stuaidh the Irish name for the townland is quoted by Daibhi de Barra who live here, the remarkable double rampart Lois commands an extensive view of sea and land to the South. The outer circular is 19 to 12 feet high while the inner one is a few feet higher, another extraordinary feature is a well within the Lois enclosure. The gateway to the Lois would have been on the South side, another Lois called Da Bhan was close by but is now demolished, and the remains of a forge called Broe's Forge are also in this part of the townland
